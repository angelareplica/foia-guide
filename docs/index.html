What is a public record?

Public records are documents created or retained by a government. If it came in contact with a government employee or government money at any point, it's probably a public record.

The types of records below can all be requested through FOIA.

  • Emails & texts
  • Telephone logs
  • Contracts
  • Photographs
  • Meeting minutes
  • Algorithms
  • Training materials
  • Receipts
  • Calendars
  • Notes & memoranda
  • Surveillance footage
  • Reports
  • Salaries
  • Military records
  • FBI files

Who makes FOIA requests?

Commercial businesses are the most prolific FOIA users, followed by private individuals and law firms. News organizations are estimated to make under 10% of all FOIA requests.

Source: FoiaMapper, 2017

State open records laws

Every state has its own open records and open meetings laws. Hover for more information, and click for a detailed public records guide.

SOURCE: MuckRock
Most states specify response deadlines ranging between 3 days and 15 days. Some states have no limit.
Completing requests typically takes state agencies several weeks.
On average, state agencies still respond much faster to records requests than federal agencies.
The federal government's track record is worse: On average, it has taken 185 days for agencies to respond to FOIA requests submitted through MuckRock.
